Background

Recently there has been local and national news coverage about shortages in primary care providers. For example, in this NPR story on the national perspective the host is quated as saying "...we don't have enough of the kinds of doctors we want in as many places as they're needed."

 

Locally here in Santa Barbara, the KSBY-TV reporter says "If you've called the doctor's office to schedule an appointment as a new patient recently, you've probably been told the wait time to get in will be several weeks or even months."

  • KSBY February 9, 2023 - Can't get in to see the doctor? Why there's a physician shortage and what's being done about it

Both stories cite supporting sources of data, AAMC for the NPR story and KFF for the KSBY story. I decided to use this context as inspiration to conduct my own analysis using publicly available data.
